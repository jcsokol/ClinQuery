# **HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS**
52-year-old male with history of obesity (BMI ~30), hyperlipidemia, daily tobacco use, and osteoarthritis of the hip, presented to the ED 4 days ago with progressive dyspnea, productive cough, and increased lower extremity edema. He initially reported a 2-week history of viral upper respiratory symptoms (congestion, sore throat), which he attributed to his chronic sinusitis and viral pharyngitis. Over the past 48 hours prior to admission, he developed severe orthopnea, paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea, and chest tightness. In the ED, he was tachypneic, hypoxic (SpO2 88% on RA), tachycardic (HR 116), and hypertensive (BP 168/94). CXR showed pulmonary congestion; BNP elevated at 2380. Troponin mildly elevated (0.09 → 0.13). Echo revealed new severe LV systolic dysfunction (EF 18%) with global hypokinesis. He was transferred to the CICU for management of suspected new-onset decompensated heart failure, likely viral myocarditis.

On hospital day 2, patient developed acute hypotension, worsening renal function, and rising lactate. Decision made for temporary mechanical circulatory support with placement of a percutaneous left ventricular assist device (Impella CP). Patient stabilized hemodynamically, with improvement in urine output and mentation. Over subsequent days, he has been weaned off vasopressors but remains on low-dose inotropes.
