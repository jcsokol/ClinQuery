## HOSPITAL COURSE SUMMARY

Mr. Guillermo Trantow is a 46-year-old male with a past medical history of hypertension, prediabetes, anemia, and recent acute bronchitis and viral sinusitis who presented to the ED with 4 days of progressive dyspnea, orthopnea, and lower extremity edema. On arrival, he was tachycardic, hypertensive, and hypoxic. Initial labs and imaging revealed acute decompensated heart failure with severely reduced ejection fraction (EF 15%), evidence of end-organ hypoperfusion, and acute kidney injury. He rapidly developed cardiogenic shock requiring transfer to the cardiac ICU, initiation of vasopressors, and placement of a temporary left ventricular assist device (Impella CP) for circulatory support.

He is currently on day 16 of admission, completing antibiotics for pneumonia, and being evaluated for advanced heart failure therapies.
